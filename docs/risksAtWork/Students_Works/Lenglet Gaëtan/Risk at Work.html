Introduction #

This page presents critical safety risks in two essential domains: industrial work and information technology. Both environments pose unique hazards that require awareness, proper training, and preventive measures. Use the interactive exercise in the Help menu to test your knowledge.

Industrial Domain – 5 Key Risks #

Mechanical Hazards

Industrial environments often involve machines with moving parts. Without proper guarding, maintenance, or training, workers can face crushing, cutting, or entanglement injuries.

Chemical Exposure

Many industrial sites use solvents, cleaning agents, or other hazardous substances. Poor ventilation or insufficient protective equipment can lead to burns, poisoning, or long-term health issues.

Electrical Risks

Faulty wiring, damaged equipment, or improper handling of electrical systems can result in shocks, fires, or explosions. Regular inspections and strict safety procedures are essential.

Ergonomic Strain

Heavy lifting, repetitive motions, or awkward postures can cause musculoskeletal disorders. Proper workstation setup and training help reduce these risks.

Fire and Explosion Hazards

Flammable materials, overheating equipment, or mishandled energy sources can trigger fires. Safety protocols, detection systems, and emergency plans are crucial.

IT Domain – 5 Key Risks #

Cybersecurity Threats

IT environments face risks such as malware, phishing, and data breaches. Without strong security measures, sensitive information can be stolen or compromised.

Data Loss

Hardware failure, software errors, or improper backup procedures can lead to the loss of critical data. Regular backups and redundancy solutions are essential.

Ergonomic Issues

Long hours at a computer can lead to back pain, eye strain, and repetitive strain injuries. Proper seating, screen positioning, and regular breaks help prevent these problems.

Network Downtime

System outages caused by technical failures, cyberattacks, or configuration errors can severely disrupt operations. Monitoring and preventive maintenance reduce this risk.

Electrical and Overheating Risks

Servers, computers, and network equipment generate heat. Poor ventilation or overloaded circuits can cause overheating, fire hazards, or equipment failure.

Prevention #

Key strategies for workplace safety:

  • Training: Ensure all employees receive proper safety training
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Provide appropriate equipment for each role
  • Regular Audits: Conduct periodic safety assessments
  • Ergonomics: Optimize workstations and work procedures
  • Emergency Plans: Develop and practice evacuation procedures
  • Maintenance: Keep equipment in proper working condition
